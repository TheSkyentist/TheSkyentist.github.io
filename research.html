At Steward Observatory I work with the JWST NIRCam Extragalactic Science Team to prepare for the upcoming GTO Survey JADES. JWST will allow us to robustly detect and collect statistics for galaxies present in the era when the universe was less than a billion year old.
As JWST is still being tested on the ground, I work with the JAGUAR galaxy mock catalog. The presence of neutral hydrogen in the neutral hydrogen absorbs most of all the light bluewards of the Lyman Alpha line. As the rest frame position of this cutoff changes with redshift, searching for "LyA dropouts" is therefore a robust way of searching for high-redshift galaxies. I have worked on calibrating potential color-color cuts for upcoming surveys with JWST and the numbers of high redshift galaxies that can be accurately recovered with this technique.
In addition, I have been working to add higher order morphological components to the JAGUAR catalog objects that go beyond a SÃ©rsic profile to reflect the expected properties of high redshift galaxies. This will culminate in the development of tools that allow for the fast and accurate extraction of higher order morphological parameters from JWST NIRCam images.
